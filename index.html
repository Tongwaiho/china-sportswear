01 · Five things to know before any China conversation

The 30-second version

$11.3B vs $6.6B

Anta Group passed Nike China in 2022 — and the gap is now huge

Anta Group 2025 revenue ≈US$11.3B (¥80.2B, +13.3%) vs Nike Greater China US$6.59B (FY25, −13%, native currency). But at brand level Nike is still China's #1 single brand (16.2% share, Euromonitor 2024). Both statements are true — which one you cite changes the story. REPORTED

国潮 ≠ answer

"Guochao" explains almost nothing about who won

Its biggest supposed beneficiary, Li-Ning, was the domestic loser of 2022–24. The foreign brand that recovered, Adidas, did so partly by executing localization better than Li-Ning sustained it. The durable variables: channel control, inventory cycles, portfolio structure. ANALYST VIEW

8,343 → 5,020

Nike's paid-for reach in China is down ~40%

Topsports (滔搏), Nike's biggest China partner, cut its store fleet ~40% between FY2019 and FY2025 — and now also sells HOKA and Li-Ning. Nike's "wholesale problem" in China is really a partner-economics problem Nike created itself. REPORTED

26.3%

China is now Adidas's most profitable region on earth

Greater China operating margin 26.3% (2025) vs 10.1% in North America — built on full-price discipline, a 45-day local product cycle, and real P&L autonomy for the China team. This is the playbook Nike announced only in 2025–26. REPORTED

Same words,
different market

"Wholesale" and "DTC" don't mean what they mean in the US

China wholesale = mono-brand partner stores (Topsports, Pou Sheng), not Foot Locker. China DTC includes brand-run Tmall/Douyin flagships. Misreading these two words is the fastest way to sound naive. See the DTC decoder. ANALYST VIEW

03 · Offline footprint, 2019–2025

Store counts: the quiet story is Nike's shrinking reach

These series count different things — read the legend carefully. Anta and Li-Ning report their own store networks. Nike and Adidas don't disclose China store counts, so their partners' fleets are the best proxy for offline reach.

Anta = Anta-brand stores incl. Kids (FILA shown separately; both REPORTED). Li-Ning = total POS incl. LI-NING YOUNG REPORTED. Adidas = total doors incl. franchise, two anchor points only (~12,000 in 2019/20 → ~7,500 in Sept 2024, exec statements) ESTIMATE. How to read this: domestic brands kept ~7,600–12,000 doors roughly stable while the two partners who physically carry Nike and Adidas closed ~5,900 stores between them (Topsports −40%, Pou Sheng −44%, FY2019–FY2025). International brands' offline distribution in China is contracting every year, concentrated in the tier-2/3/4 cities where domestic brands are strongest. Meanwhile Topsports hedged by adding HOKA, Li-Ning and Asics to its portfolio.

04 · Revenue by channel

Channel mix: what's disclosed, what isn't, and why the 2×2 doesn't exist

The intuitive grid — offline DTC / online DTC / offline wholesale / online wholesale — cannot be filled from disclosures. Brands report three buckets, not four, and each company cuts them differently. That mismatch is itself an insight: online wholesale is real but invisible — distributor-run Nike stores on Tmall/Douyin sit inside "wholesale" and are never broken out.

05 · The word that will trip you up

DTC decoder: three different things wear the same name

In the US, "DTC" has one meaning. In China it has at least three — and a sell-side research house will use it differently from Nike's own 10-K. Get this wrong and every channel conversation that follows is built on sand.

US / Nike accounting

DTC = brand.com + brand-owned stores

Nike Direct in the 10-K: NIKE-owned retail plus digital platforms. In China this includes Nike-operated Tmall/JD/Douyin flagships and the WeChat mini-program — Nike is merchant of record on those marketplaces. Partner-run mono-brand stores are booked as wholesale. REPORTED

China / consumer-facing reality

"DTC-style" retail executed by partners

What shoppers experience as a "Nike store" in most Chinese cities is owned and staffed by Topsports or Pou Sheng under Nike's format rules. The DTC experience is delivered through wholesale economics. Brands directly run only concept stores (House of Innovation, Nike Style, Rise) and factory outlets. ANALYST VIEW

Anta's version

DTC = buying back some franchisees — not all of them

Aug 2020: Anta terminated 11 regional distributors covering ~3,500 stores (~35% of its fleet, ~¥2.0B — mostly inventory buyback), converting ~60% of those specific stores to direct operation. But group-wide, Anta's own annual reports say only ~44% of stores under its "DTC model" are directly staffed by Anta — the rest remain franchisee-run under Anta's standards. "DTC" here means centrally controlled, not necessarily self-operated. Gross margin still rose ~41% → ~52% in two years. REPORTED

Nike Greater China: NIKE Direct share of revenue (10-K, reported)
The plateau is the point: GC Direct share peaked FY23 at 46.7% and Nike has since rebuilt wholesale (FY24–25 ~43–44%). Changjiang Securities notes Adidas's CEO called 35–40% DTC "the right level" for retailer relationships, and Nike re-embraced Foot Locker / DSW / Macy's from 2023 to clear inventory. The global DTC-maximalist era is over — in both markets. ANALYST VIEW
The sell-side view (长江证券, Topsports initiation, May 2024): "Unlike Europe and the US — where retailers are mainly multi-brand chains and brands' DTC means own stores and official sites — in China, retailers like Topsports and Pou Sheng open mono-brand stores for international brands, which themselves run only a few concept and factory stores. So in China, international brands' DTC strategy is in practice executed through these retailers — making quality distributors like Topsports irreplaceable."
原文: "不同于欧美市场，国内市场主要为滔搏、宝胜等零售商开设海外品牌单品牌店为主……因而国内市场DTC主要通过滔搏与宝胜等零售商贯彻执行，因而滔搏此类优质经销商未来仍不可替代。" — 于旭辉、柯睿，长江证券《滔滔逐潮往，搏浪奋辑行》2024-05-21 ANALYST VIEW
How that claim has aged (two years on): half vindicated, half challenged. Vindicated: Elliott Hill is rebuilding wholesale relationships, and nobody built a direct fleet to replace the partners — sinking-market retail is exactly the heavy-asset, low-margin work global brands can't do in China. Challenged: "irreplaceable" didn't mean "protected" — Topsports has since shrunk further to 4,360 doors (FY2026, latest reported), profit fell ~42%, it diversified into HOKA/Li-Ning/Asics, and Nike is reportedly cutting its online sales rights from 2027. The partner layer is irreplaceable as infrastructure but no longer safe as a business. That distinction matters for anyone designing Nike's China retail future.

06 · Same wave, different fates

Four mechanisms behind the tipping points

Each shock hit all four brands. Who bled and who gained was decided by channel structure, inventory position, and price-brand fit — not by sentiment. These are the stories behind the markers on the revenue chart, with analyst reasoning separated from fact.

Guochao didn't pick the winners — channel and pricing did

FACT Li-Ning was guochao's flagship: 中国李宁 streetwear, +56.1% revenue in 2021. Yet 2022–24 it became the domestic laggard: 2024 revenue +3.9% with net profit −5.5%, the weakest of China's big four; brokers cut profit forecasts ~25%.

ANALYST VIEW 壹览商业's autopsy: "what supported Li-Ning's price hikes was guochao sentiment, not R&D" — LI-NING 1990 coats reached ¥4,599, near Nike/Adidas territory, without the brand power to hold it. The 中国李宁 line pushed the company into a hit-driven fashion arena it wasn't built for, while Anta competed through a portfolio of separately positioned brands.

ANALYST VIEW The channel made it worse: with ~48% of revenue in wholesale, cash-strapped distributors dumped inventory online at uncontrolled discounts while Li-Ning simultaneously ran premium marketing — 派财经 calls it the "raise prices upstairs, fire-sale downstairs" loop (一边涨价搞品牌、一边打折清库存). Anta HQ decisions reach direct stores same-day; Li-Ning's take days through franchisees, if they comply at all.

FACT Oct 2022's 逐梦行 jacket controversy (−15% stock in a week) showed guochao equity is reversible — but the margin decay predates it by three quarters. Meanwhile the foreign "guochao winner" was… Adidas: 新浪's 2025 recovery piece is literally titled "阿迪达斯翻身，靠的竟然是'国潮'".

Sources: 壹览商业 · CBNData: 中国李宁救不了李宁 · 派财经 · 物流指闻: DTC地基效应 · 知微数据: 逐梦行舆情 · 新浪财经 · 证券时报
Nike's decline is substantially self-inflicted — Xinjiang was the trigger, not the cause

FACT Nike GC peaked FY21 ($8.29B), then fell in five of six years to $5.85B (FY26). Domestic brands grew through the same lockdown quarters — the demand didn't vanish, it moved (The China Project ran the numbers in 2022).

ANALYST VIEW Mechanism 1 — classic-franchise overdraft: AF1/Dunk/AJ1 were over-supplied to hit quarterly targets; when the sneaker-flipping wave broke, they became the inventory pile. The 得物 resale bubble Nike's scarcity marketing created then collapsed — Panda Dunk went from ~¥2,000 resale to below its ¥699 retail. Discounting to clear it eroded exactly the premium image the model depended on: the 折扣→毛利→品牌力→库存 doom loop.

ANALYST VIEW Mechanism 2 — CDA gutted partner economics: Donahoe's Consumer Direct Acceleration deliberately downgraded distributors; Topsports shrank from 8,343 (FY19) to 5,020 doors (FY25), a ~40% cut, and now distributes HOKA and Li-Ning. 界面新闻's verdict: "DTC isn't the original sin — losing product innovation is" (DTC不是原罪，产品失去创新才是根本).

ANALYST VIEW Mechanism 3 — carved up by specialists, not beaten by one rival: runners to HOKA/On/特步, hikers to Arc'teryx/Salomon (both Anta-affiliated), fashion back to Adidas Samba. Nike merchandised by gender while the market shifted to scenario/function.

FACT Elliott Hill's own words: "We have become a casual lifestyle brand competing on price in China." The fix announced: China-designed, China-made product by holiday 2027, inventory pulled down (GC units −20%+ in Q3 FY26), 100 owned-store pilot, margin over volume. The effect so far is mixed: Q3 FY26 GC EBIT rose 11% on that discipline — but the full fiscal year still closed down 20% in GC operating income ($1.60B → $1.28B), because revenue fell faster than costs came out. The reset is a multi-year bet, not a fixed quarter.

Sources: The China Project · CBNData: Dunk 泡沫 · 虎嗅 · 界面新闻 · 华福证券: 复盘Nike DTC · 新浪科技 · Nike Q3 FY26 call
Anta's rise = an inventory machine plus a portfolio machine (and both now face stress)

FACT The 2020 DTC buyback (3,500 stores, 11 regions) was framed by 光大证券 at the time as securing demand-feedback speed and inventory reallocation — FILA's 2009–12 full-direct conversion was the template (store turns 0.8→1.5×/yr, inventory days 180→90). Anta brand gross margin: ~41% (2019) → ~52% (2021).

ANALYST VIEW When FILA stalled (−1.4% in 2022, its first decline), the group replaced the engine on schedule: 妙投/虎嗅 call the real capability "批量造FILA" — a repeatable brand-operating system. 2025: outdoor brands +59.2%; Descente past ¥10B retail sales; Kolon ~+70%; Jack Wolfskin acquired to fill the mass-price outdoor band; Arc'teryx (via non-consolidated Amer) past $2B alone. "Anta rising" in 2025 really means "Descente/Kolon/Arc'teryx rising."

FACT The watchouts are structural, not cyclical: main Anta brand +3.7% (2025) and losing share of group; group inventory days at 137, stock +13% to ¥12.2B. DTC means Anta now owns the inventory risk it used to push onto distributors — the machine that made it also concentrates the exposure.

Sources: 光大证券研报 (2020) · 妙投/虎嗅 7千字解析 · Anta FY2025 results (IR) · 虎嗅 2025年报 · 投中网: 主品牌掉队 · 观察者网: 狼爪
Adidas fell harder than Nike — then wrote the localization playbook Nike is now copying

FACT 2022: GC revenue −36% currency-neutral, far deeper than Nike's −9%. CEO Rorsted's exit admission: "We don't understand consumers well enough, so we left room for Chinese competitors who are better off." Weeks later he was gone; Yeezy termination then removed a major profit pool.

ANALYST VIEW Why deeper: one global product line with zero China-specific product, heavier physical/wholesale exposure, weaker e-commerce, and a fashion-skewed mix (Yeezy/Superstar hype) most vulnerable to taste shifts.

FACT The recovery mechanics under Gulden: 2023 inventory clean-out first; then full-price discipline in a promotional market; China P&L and product autonomy under Adrian Siu (萧家乐) — idea-to-shelf in 45 days vs the old 9-month global review, "created in China" >80% of range; Samba/T-toe wave (+20× in 2023, ~€1.5B in 2024) as the product cycle on top. Result: 10+ consecutive growth quarters and a 26.3% GC operating margin — the highest of any Adidas region (North America: 10.1%).

ANALYST VIEW The caveat analysts flag: the retro wave is cooling (界面: "除了Samba，凭什么?") — the recovery must hand off from fashion to running/performance to hold. And note the uncomfortable symmetry: Adidas recovered by doing guochao-style localization with discipline; that was supposed to be Li-Ning's home turf.

Sources: China.Table: Rorsted quote · Athletic Interest: How Adidas lost China · Adidas AR 2025 (GC segment) · 新浪: 阿迪缓过来了 · 品牌星球 · 界面: 除了Samba
The 2022 inventory crisis split the market into a barbell — and the middle never came back

FACT 2022 was industry-wide and measurable: gross margins fell at all four domestic majors (Anta −1.4pp, Li-Ning −4.6pp — the worst); inventory days rose across the board (Anta 127→138, Li-Ning 54→58). Nike and Adidas cleared through outlets and livestream discounting.

ANALYST VIEW 妙投's framing: "the industry's fate line is the inventory cycle" (运动鞋服行业的命门即库存周期) — DTC and supply-chain digitization exist to manage it, full stop. Channel structure decided who bled: Anta's direct control let it discount surgically; Li-Ning's wholesale rails dumped stock and wrecked price integrity; Nike/Adidas bled brand equity because their prices were built on scarcity.

FACT Demand then split, it didn't just shrink: high-performance outdoor grew ¥53.9B (2019) → ¥102.7B (2024, 13.8% CAGR) — Arc'teryx +86%, Salomon +73%, HOKA/On +50%+ online — at the same time as 性价比 value consumption boomed. The squeezed middle is precisely where big-logo Nike lifestyle product and mainline Li-Ning were positioned; Li-Ning raised prices into the collapsing middle, on wholesale rails. That — not the 逐梦行 jacket — is why guochao "didn't save" it.
